About Ugo

Japanese traditional tattoo, rooted in reverence.

Tradition as Living Art

Ugo is a Japanese traditional and neo-traditional tattoo artist based at Guru Tattoo in San Diego, California — one of the most respected private studios on the West Coast. His work draws from the deep wellspring of Irezumi: the mythological narratives, natural motifs, and spiritual symbolism that have defined Japanese tattooing for centuries.

His subject matter speaks an ancient language — Hannya masks that embody jealousy and sorrow transformed, peonies that symbolize wealth and bravery, cranes that carry prayers between worlds. But his execution is unmistakably contemporary: rich teal and violet compositions, burnt orange and gold highlights, all flowing with the natural architecture of the human body.

"The body is not a flat canvas. Every muscle, every curve tells you where the image wants to live. My job is to listen."

Ugo specializes in large-scale work — full sleeves, back pieces, and leg sleeves that transform the body into a unified narrative. His compositions are known for their masterful flow, where mythological figures and botanical elements wrap and move with the wearer rather than simply sitting on the surface.

An international presence, Ugo has tattooed at Mondial du Tatouage in Paris, the Winnipeg Tattoo Convention, and guest spots across North America and Europe. His convention work has earned recognition for pieces that honor the weight and gravity of traditional Japanese imagery while commanding a distinctly modern palette and presence.

Whether working in his signature rich color or delivering stunning black and grey compositions, every piece begins with research, reference, and conversation — a collaborative process that ensures each tattoo carries personal meaning alongside cultural respect.
